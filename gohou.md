
# 語法について

## 語彙

- cop: 警官
- organizer: 主催者
- participant: 参加者
- clerk: 店員

## TELL 型

- 以下の３つの型が存在
- V3: tell 人 / of 物 (人に物について伝える)
- V4: tell 人 [that SV] (人にthat~と伝える)
- V5: tell 人 {to V} (人に〜*するように*伝える)

### tell

- He told him team / of the new generative AI tools / that can enhance productivity.
- I told her / [that I wanted to see her / again].
- I told him / {to sig up online}.

### `remind (思い出させる)`

- This fog reminds me of the weather in London.
- The app reminds users that they should drink water every hour.
- Please remind me / {to stop / by an ATM / on our way home}. ~することを思い出させる
  - `be kindly reminded (~をどうぞご承知おきください / どうか〜してください)`
    - The audience is kindly reminded that photography is not permitted during te performance.

### convince (納得/確信させる) [toV は 納得させてVさせる]

- My dentist convinced me of the importance of flossing every day.
- She convinced her boss that remote work increases productivity.
- My sister convinced me to help her with her homework.

### persuade (説得する) [toV は説得してVさせる]

- of~ の形は稀
- You will never persuade me that your idea is better than mine.
  - 決してthat~だとあなたは私を説得させられない --> 君がなんと言おうとthat~だとは思わない
- I tried / {to persuade my parents / {to by me a computer}}.

### warn (警告する) [toV は Vするように警告する]

- The scientist warned the community of the harmful effects of air pollution.
- The waiter warned us that the restaurant would be closing in 15 minutes.
- After the cop gave Andy a speeding ticket, she wanted him not to drive too fast again.
- The scientific report warns of many disasters that could result from global warming.
  - `結果 result from 原因 (結果は原因によって生じる)`

### notify (知らせる)

- The university / will notify / all examinees / of their results / by mail.
  - by mail: 郵送で
  - examinee: 受験者
- Jill was overjoyed when she was notified that she had won the contest.
  - be overjoyed: 大喜びする
- to V は稀

### advise (助言する・忠告する・知らせる) [toV は強制・警告のニュアンス]

- The event organizers advised the participant of the schedule change.
- The shop clerk advised us that the sale ended today.
- Her lawyer advised her not to say anything.
- `人 is advised to V (人はVするように忠告される --> 人はVするようにご注意ください)`
- `人 is advised that SV (SVをご了承ください))`
  - Please be advised / that the meeting / has been postponed / due to unforeseen circumstances.
    - due to unforeseen circumstances: 不測の事態により
    - unforeseen: 不測の

### assure (保証する・確信させる・安心させる)

- The supervisor assured the workers of the safety of the machine.
- My doctor assured me that I would start feeling better within 24 hours.
  - within は ~以内に(期限を超えないうちに)。 in は ~後に(ほぼキッカリ、またはその直前に)

### inform (知らせる)

- The company informed customers of the price increase by email.
- The school administration informed students that new ID cards would be issued in April.
  - issue: 発行する
- バリエーション
  - `be informed of ~ について知らされている`
    - Employees were informed of the new policy in a memo from the CEO.
      - memo: 社内連絡
  - `keep informed of ~ について常に知らされ続ける --> 常に把握しておく`
    - It is necessary to keep informed of market trends to make better investment decisions.
  - `keep me informed of ~ について常に知っている`
    - Please keep me informed about any change of plans.
    - I'll keep you informed
    - `keep me posted / keep me updated on/about ~`
      - Please keep me posted on your progress.
      - post / update: 最新情報を伝える

## SAY 型

- `say to 人 [that]` の形が基本。人に、という時には 前置詞 to が必須
- to 前置詞句は副詞句なので大抵は V3 で使用。ただし `apologize to 人 for 理由` の `apologize` は V1。 `apologize to 人 that SV` は V3 で「that~だと謝る」

### say

- The educator said to the school board that online learning has many benefits.
  - school board: 教育委員会
  - board は組織のトップが集まって行う重要な会議。取締役会。meeting はただの人が集まった会議や打ち合わせ。

### suggest (仄めかす・提案する)

- Please suggest to us some place to visit in Los Angeles.

### explain (説明する)

- I explained to my supervisor that I needed a day off for a doctor's appointment.

### apologize (謝る)

- `apologize to 人 that SV (人にthat~ だと謝る)`
  - Roger apologized to everyone that he wasn't able to attend the meeting.
- `apologize(v1) to 人 for 理由 (理由のことで人に謝る)`
  - The airline apologized to passengers for the cancellation of flights due to technical issues.
    - due to ~ は the cancellation を修飾

### mention (言及する)

- Do you remember that I mentioned to you yesterday that I'm looking for a new job?

### announce (公表する)

- I announced to my friends that my family was moving to Fukuoka.

### admit (白状する)

- Takahiro admitted to us that he hadn't slept at all the night before the exam.
- `admit Ving (Vしたことを白状する)`
  - She admitted having driven the car without insurance.

### express (表現する)

- I'd like to express my gratitude to you for everything you've done.
  - gratitude: 感謝

### propose (提案する)

- We proposed a new marketing strategy to our clients.

### declare (宣言する)

- The archaeologist declared to the journalists that she had discovered the ruins of an ancient temple.
  - ruins: 遺跡
  - archaeologist: 考古学者

### confess (打ち明ける)

- She confessed to her boss that she didn't think his jokes were funny.

## ROB 型

- `rob(3) 人 of 物` が基本
- 人を襲って物から分離させる がコアのイメージ

### rob

- The pandemic has robbed millions of workers of their employment, leading to economic hardship.
  - The pandemic が millions of workers を襲って their employment を奪い去った
  - employment: 雇用
  - `原因 lead to 結果`: The carbon tax will inevitably lead to an increase in energy bills.

### deprive (奪う)

- The children were deprived of sleep due to the noise.

### cure (治す) [cure 人 of 病気]

- The doctor cured the patient of pneumonia(肺炎).
- Digital detox programs are designed to cure users of their social media addiction.
  - addiction: 中毒、依存症
  - to cure は are designed の目的を表す ~ように設計されている

### clear (片付ける) [clear 人 of 物]

- Scientists are working to clear Earth's orbit of space debris.
  - orbit: 軌道
  - debris: 破片、ゴミ

### ease (楽にする・安心させる) [ease 人 of 苦痛な物]

- The massage eased her of the tension in her shoulders.
  - tension: こり

### strip (剥ぎ取る)

### empty (空にする) [empty A of B (A の中身を、Bを取り出すことによって空にする)]

- I emptied my pockets of change and walked through the metal detector again.
  - metal detector: 金属探知機

### relieve (安心させる) [relieve 人 (of 不安)]

- Your kind words have relieved me of my fears.

### rid (奪う)

- Vaccines have rid most of the world of polio.
  - polio: 小児麻痺
- `be rid of 不快な人・物 (~がいなくなって)`
  - She's glad to be rid of her controlling ex-boyfriend.
  - 不愉快な物・人物がいなくなって
  - controlling: 支配的な
- `get rid of 物 (ものを取り除く) [be rid of の状態にする(get)]`
  - get rid of one's bad habit / get rid of this bad cold
  - Ecologists are urging the public to get rid of single-use plastics to reduce ocean pollution.
    - urge 人 to V: 人にVするように促す
    - ecologists: 環境保護論者
    - single-use: 使い捨ての
    - ocean pollution: 海洋汚染

## PROVIDE 型

- `provide(3) 人 with 物` で 「人に物を与える」が基本
- 人に物を付け加えるイメージ
- `provide`, `supply` は `V 物 for/to 人` の形が取れる
- `furnish`, `feed`, `present` は`V 物 to 人` の形が取れる
- 他の動詞は `V 人 with 物` の形のみ

### provide (提供する)

- NGOs provided refugees with food, clothing, and shelter to help them rebuild their lives.

### supply (供給する)

- My acquaintances supply me with very useful information.
  - acquaintance: 知り合い

### furnish (与える) [家具に限らない]

- We are happy to furnish you with the information you are looking for.

### feed (与える) [食事に限らない]

- Parents are feeding their children with positive affirmation.
  - positive affirmation: 前向きなことば
- `be fed up with ~ (~にうんざりしている)`
  - upは強調。with~ を十分与えられている --> うんざりしている
  - People are fed up with all these traffic jams.

### present (贈呈する)

- She was presented with an award for her contributions to the firm.
  - `contribution to ~`: 〜への貢献

### fill (満たす)

- Excited children filled the room with laughter.

### load A with B (AにBを詰め込む)

- They loaded the boxes with oranges.
- `load A into/onto B (AにBを載せる)`

### face (直面させる)

- We were faced with two alternatives: keep going or give up.
  - alternative: 代わりの別のもの、代替。ただし two alternative といった場合は互いに代替の状態、つまりAの代わりがB,Bの代わりがAという状態、互いに排他的な選択肢、二者択一という意味合いが出る。

### confront (直面させる)

- The company is confronted with a major financial crisis.
  - financial crisis: 財政危機

### equip (備え付ける) [equip A with B (AにBを)]

- The car was equipped with a state-of-the-art navigation system.
  - state-of-the-art: 「ある分野の技術(art)が現在到達している最高水準(state)」で、「今のところ最も進んだ段階 --> 最先端の」

### endow 人 with 才能 [人に才能を与える --> 人が才能に恵まれている]

- The new CEO was endowed with exceptional leadership skills

### acquaint 人 with 物 (人に物を知るきっかけを与える)

- `get acquainted with ~ (~と知り合いになる)`
- Through social media, it's easier to get acquainted with people who share your interests.

### charge 人 with 大事な仕事など (委ねる)

- I was charged with the task of organizing the company picnic.

## THANK 型

- `thank(3) 人 for 理由 (理由のことで人に感謝する)` が基本。
- 感謝型 `thank`, `praise`, `admire`, `respect`, `commend`
- `to 人` になる型: `be grateful/thankful to 人 for 理由`, `be obliged to 人 for 理由`, `apologize to 人 for 理由`
- 避難系: `blame`, `criticize`, `s`

### thank

- I thanked him for explaining the procedure to me.

### praise (褒める)

- The young mother praised the baby for not crying.

### admire (感嘆する)

- Many Japanese people admire that novelist for winning the prize.
  - win the prize: 受賞する

### respect (尊敬する)

- We respect you for all you've accomplished.

### commend (称賛する)

- She is highly commended for her contributions to cancer research.

### be grateful/thankful to 人 for 理由 (感謝している)

- Community members are grateful to the local firefighters for containing the recent wildfire.
  - contain は 含む --> 中に入れる --> 封じ込める という意味になる
  - wildfire: 山火事
- We are thankful to the doctor for saving our child's life.
- `I would be grateful if you could/would ~ (~していただけるとありがたいのですが)`
  - I would be grateful if you would consider my application for the position.
    - consider: ご検討
    - application: 応募
    - position: 仕事の職

### be obliged to 人 for 理由 (感謝している・恩義を受けている)

- I'm much obliged to my mentor for guiding my career.
- `be obliged to V (やむを得ずVする, Vせざるをえない)`
  - Parents are obliged by law to send their children to school.

### apologize to 人 for 理由

- I sincerely apologize to you for not informing you about the changes earlier.
  - sincerely: 心から

### blame (非難する) [for 理由 の責任の所在を 人 だとする] [責任の帰属]

- His teammates blamed him for losing the game.
  - his teammates は losing the game したことの責任の所在を him とした

### criticize (批判・非難する) [欠点/不満の表明]

- The mayor was criticized for not listening to residents' needs.
- criticize はデフォルトでは 「欠点を指摘して不承認を示す」という意味。「欠点を指摘する」ところに意味の重点がある。`constructive criticize` や学術・批評系文脈では中立になる可能性があるが、やはり欠点を指摘するところはかわらない。この意味で中立な批評をする場合は `critique` を用いる.
- `blame`は責任の所在を特定する。
- `condemn` は 道徳的判断。
- `denounce` は公の告発。

### condemn (非難する) [道徳的に許されない、これはあってはならないことだと強く断じる] [道徳的判断]

- Salespeople at the company were condemned for deceiving customers.
  - deceive: 騙す

### denounce (公然と非難する) [誰が悪いのかを公の場で名指しし告発する]

- The media denounced the actor for his inappropriate comments.

### scold (叱る)

- The dog was scolded by its owner for stealing food from the table.

### punish (罰する)

- The student was punished for using AI to write their essay without permission.

### fine (罰金を課す) [be fined(-4) 金額 for 理由]

- 金額がある場合は [be fined(-4) 金額 for 理由]
- 金額がない場合は [be fined(-3) for 理由]
- The restaurant was fined $1,000 for being unsanitary.
  - unsanitary: 不衛生な

### prosecute (起訴する)

- The police prosecuted the boys for trespassing.
  - trespassing: 不法侵入

### suspend (停職/停学にする)

- The student was suspended for three days for cheating on an exam.

## PERSUADE 型

- `persuade 人 into Ving` は 「人を説得してVさせる」
- `persuade 人 out of Ving` は 「人を説得してVをやめさせる」

### persuade (説得する)

- 説得して〜させるは `persuade 人 to V` をよく使う。また「やめさせる」は `persuade 人 out of N` の形ならよく使う
- 説得して〜させる、〜をやめさせる は `talk`の方がよくつかわれる
- I tried to persuade her out of her plan to move abroad, but she was determined to go.

### talk (説得する)

- 通常は自動詞だが直後に人が来るとV3で説得するという意味になる
- The salesperson talked him into buying the extended warranty for his smartphone.
- She talked herself out of buying a new pair of shoes.
- I was talked out of investing in that risky startup by my business partner.

### trick (騙して~させる)

- The man tricked her into thinking he was her son.
- `cheat`, `fool`, `deceive`, `con` も同じ形で同じ意味
- The pitcher fooled the runner into thinking he didn't have the ball.
  - "Did you hear about her recent business deal? -- Yeah. She got conned into investing lots of money."
  - business dail： 商取引, 商談

### bully (いじめて~させる)

- The boy bullied two classmates into letting him copy from them during the test.

### force (強制して~させる)

- Rising expenses have forced us into raising our prices.
  - rising expenses: 経費の高騰

### scare / frighten (怖がらせて/脅して ~させる)

- The stalker scared his victim into moving to another city.

### needle (挑発して〜させる)

- The reporters needled the spokesperson into revealing more details of the incident.
  - spokesperson: 広報担当者

## PREVENT 型

- `prevent 人 from Ving` が基本
- 「人がVするのを妨げる」 --> 「Sによって(おかげで・せいで)人は Ving しない・できない」
- from は分離を表す。人が Ving の行為から分離して --> 人は Ving しない・できない

### prevent (妨げる)

- The guard prevented me from entering the building.

### keep (妨げる)

- Her "shh!" kept him from telling the others their secret.

### stop (妨げる)

- Her timely advice stopped me from making an embarrassing mistake.
- stop は `stop Ving (Vするのをやめる)` の語法が有名

### prohibit (禁止する)

- School rules prohibit students from using phones in classrooms.

### ban (締め出す)

- Masao was banned from a social network site for sharing fake news.

### bar (妨げる・禁止する)

- Protestors are allowed to carry signs, but are barred from using megaphones.
  - carry signs: プラカードを掲げる
  - protestor: 抗議者

### inhibit (妨げる)

- High gas prices and the prospect of a long commute inhibited him from taking the job offer.
- the prospect of: ~という見込み
- a long commute: 長い通勤時間

### disable (能力を奪う) [disable 人 from 能力（人から〜する能力を奪う/出来なくする）]

- The pain in his hands completely disabled him from writing.
- `disable 人 from 能力` だが `enable 人 to V (Vを可能にする)` という語法になる

### restrain (妨げる・禁止する)

- The heavy chain restrained the dog from approaching the visitors.

### forbid 人 to V (禁止する) [禁止系で例外的に to V をとる動詞]

- `forbid(5) 人 to V` の第５文型
- `forbid(3) 人 form Ving` の第３文型の形も存在する
- My parents have forbidden me to use my phone after midnight.

### discourage (思いとどまらせる)

- His criticism discouraged me from entering the contest.
  - criticism: 批判

### dissuade (思いとどまらせる)

- persuade の反対の意味
- `persuade 人 into / out of / of / that SV / to V`
- `dissuade 人 from Ving`
- My friends dissuaded me from giving up.

### deter (思いとどまらせる)

- The dirty water deterred us from swimming in the hotel pool.

### excuse (免除する)

- People who helped set up the party where excused from doing clean up.

### save (救う・不要にする)

- Her cat's companionship saved the elderly woman from being lonely.
  - companionship: いっしょにいること
  - her cat's companionship は the elderly woman が being lonely でいることを不要にした
  - her cat's companionship は the elderly woman を being lonely でいることから救った

### rescue (救う)

- The new medication rescued patients from suffering from chronic pain.
- medication: 薬
- chronic pain: 慢性的な痛み

### guard (保護する)

- A high-tech security system guards the diamond from being stolen.

### protect (保護する)

- Regular exercise protects the body from becoming weak and susceptible to illness.
- regular exercise: 定期的な運動
- be susceptible to 病気: 病気にかかりやすい (〜の影響を受けやすい)

## THINK 型

- 以下の２つの形がある
  - `think(1) of/about N (Nについて考える)`
  - `think(3) [that SV] (that SV だと思っている)`
- `V of N` は `NについてVする`
- `V that SV` は `that SV だと Vする`

### think of/that

- Can you think of anything else we should bring?
- She thinks that the stock price will go up.

### know of/that

- Do you know of an alternate route to the airport?
- We know that you haven't been telling the truth.

### hear of/that (耳にする,話に聞いている)

- Have you heard of Vinnie's? Some people say they make the best pizza in New York City.
- Did you hear that she is being transferred?

### dream of/that

- She dreamed about winning the lottery and moving to a tropical island.
- I often dream that I can fly.

### complain of/that (不満を言う)

- Lately my grandfather has been complaining of ringing in his ears.
  - ring in one's ears: 耳鳴りがする
- Parents often complain of children spending too much time on smartphones.
  - children は spending の意味上の主語
- Teachers often complain that they have to do too many things unrelated to teaching.
- `to 人` がつくこともある
  - I complained to my neighbor about the loud music late at night.

### boast of/that (自慢する)

- Parents often boast of their children's achievements to other parents.
- My sister boasted that she was accepted to that prestigious university.
  - prestigious: 一流の
  - be accepted to: に受け入れられる
- `S boast 誇らしい物 (Sは誇らしいものを持っている)` の第３文型の形もある。
  - The tablet boasts four high quality speakers.

### be sure of/that

### be conscious of/that (気づいている)

- Are you conscious of the impact on the environment?
  - impact on~ : ~ に対する影響
- He is conscious that he needs to try harder.

### be aware of/that (気づいている)

- Are you aware of the time? We have to leave in 5 minutes.
- I was not aware that Satomi and Takashi were dating.
- `Please be aware of/that (をご承知おきください)`
  - Please be aware that all expense reports are due by the end of this week.
    - expense reports: 経費報告書
    - be due 期限: 期限が締め切りである

### be ignorant of/that (気づいていない)

- She was ignorant of the fact that she had the winning lottery ticket in her purse.
  - purse: 財布
  - winning lottery ticket: 宝くじの当選券
- George was ignorant that Mr. Petersen was planning a surprise quiz in his math class.
  - surprise quiz: 抜き打ちテスト

### be proud of/that (誇りに思っている)

### be ashamed of/that (恥ずかしく思っている)

- He was ashamed of his own laziness.
  - laziness: 怠惰
- She is ashamed that she lost her driver's license.

### be afraid of/that (怖がっている)

### be careful of/that (気を付ける)

### be convinced of/that (確信している)

### be desirous of/that (望んでいる)

### be fearful of/that (恐れている)

### be hopeful of/that (期待している)

## SUGGEST 型

- `S suggest that S V原` もしくは `S suggest that S should V原`
- 大きく命令形の動詞
  - 提案: suggest/propose/recommend
  - 主張: urge
  - 要求: request/require/demand/insist/ask
  - 命令: order/command/mandate/advise
  - 決定: decide/determine/arrange
- 名詞形でも同じことになる。つまり
  - `suggest that S V原` --> `suggestion that S V原` 抽象名詞 + 同格that
  - `suggest that S V原` --> `one's suggestion is that S V原` 補語

### suggest (原型の場合: 提案する / 直説法の場合: 仄めかす)

- Researchers suggested that the university allocate more funds for AI research.
  - funds: 資金
- The students suggested to the dean that more online courses be offered.
  - dean: 学部長
- `suggestion that sv`
  - The manager's suggestion that everyone submit their reports by Monday was well-received.
    - well-received: 評判の良い
- 直説法の suggest (仄めかす)
  - The guide suggested that we're going to the hunted house next.
  - The researcher suggested that her team **was** close to making a breakthrough.
    - be close to Ving: もう少しでVする
    - make a breakthrough: 大躍進をする
  - `研究 suggest that SV` は 「研究はthat~をほのめかす・示唆する」--> 「研究によって that~ がわかっている」
    - A lot of studies suggest that meditation reduces stress levels.

### propose (提案する)

- She proposed that we meet at 4:00 P.M.
- `proposal that S V原`
  - There was a controversial proposal that English be made the official language of the country.
    - controversial: 物議をかもす
    - official language: 公用語

### recommend (推奨する)

- The IT department recommended that all passwords be changed every 90 days.
- The doctor recommended that she not eat fatty foods.
- `recommendation that S V原` --> この場合は 「忠告・勧告」
  - The board accepted a recommendation that the company's structure be reorganized.
    - board: 取締役会
    - reorganize: 再編成する
- `one's recommendation is that S V原`
  - The safety inspector's recommendation was that all machinery be thoroughly checked monthly.
    - inspector: 検査官

## BELIEVE 型

- 以下の２つの語法を取り、かつ「認識」の意味を持つ
- `believe that SV` 「〜だと信じている」
- `believe 人 to be C` または `believe 人 C` 「人がCであると信じている」

### believe

- I believe that the movie is overrated.
- I believe the movie to be overrated.
  - overrate: 過大評価する
- I believe that she is talented. --> I believe her to be talented.
- I believe that she *was* talented. --> I believe her to *have been* talented.
